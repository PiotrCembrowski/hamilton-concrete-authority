# Local Keyword Strategy — HCCR

## 1. Audience & intent frame

Every keyword is evaluated for a **commercial B2B** searcher — a property manager, facility
manager, HOA/COA board member, or commercial owner — not a homeowner. Practical consequences:

- **Exclude** residential modifiers entirely: driveway, patio, pool deck, garage floor,
  stamped/decorative, residential sealing. These are out of scope even when high-volume.
- **Prefer** modifiers that signal a budget-holder with liability and a property: "commercial,"
  "parking lot," "HOA," "retail center," "warehouse," "loading dock," "ADA," "property
  management," "facility."
- **Geography is fixed to Hamilton County, Indiana** + the Indianapolis-metro framing. Target
  cities: Carmel, Fishers, Noblesville, Westfield, and nearby Zionsville (Boone County). Optional
  low-competition: Cicero, Sheridan, Arcadia, Atlanta (IN).

## 2. Intent tiers

| Tier | Intent | Example | Maps to |
|---|---|---|---|
| **T1 — Transactional local** | Ready to hire, geo-qualified | "commercial concrete repair Carmel IN", "loading dock repair Fishers" | City pages, service pages |
| **T2 — Transactional service** | Ready to hire, service-led (county/metro geo) | "parking lot concrete repair Hamilton County", "ADA sidewalk compliance Indianapolis" | Service pages |
| **T3 — Commercial investigation** | Comparing/scoping, pre-quote | "concrete repair vs replacement cost", "how much does commercial concrete repair cost" | Resource articles (link down to services) |
| **T4 — Informational / authority** | Researching the problem | "causes of concrete spalling", "ADA sidewalk slope requirements" | Resource articles |

Money pages (service + city) own T1/T2. Articles own T3/T4 and pass equity *down* to money
pages. This is the silo.

## 3. Prioritization model (qualitative, not fabricated)

Each target is scored on four axes, **labeled judgments — not measured data**:

- **Commercial intent** (High / Med / Low): how clearly the searcher is a paying commercial buyer.
- **Ranking difficulty** (qualitative): reasoned from query competitiveness and the brand's
  current (low) authority — a real SAB with no reviews/citations yet should expect city-level
  and long-tail service terms to be winnable first; broad metro head terms later.
- **Expected lead value** (High / Med / Low): typical project size/margin behind the query
  (loading dock, replacement, parking-lot > single sidewalk panel).
- **Relevance** to existing scope and inventory.

**Priority bands:**

- **P0 — build/fix now:** high intent × winnable × high lead value, and supports an existing
  money page. (City pages, the 9 service rewrites, the Cost Guide.)
- **P1 — near-term:** strong intent, slightly harder or lower volume; net-new service pages and
  the remaining articles.
- **P2 — later:** optional cities, lower-demand net-new pages, broad metro head terms that need
  authority first.

## 4. Why local/long-tail first (reasoned)

The business is a Service-Area Business with, today, **no verifiable NAP, no Google Business
Profile, no reviews, and no citations** (see audit). Broad head terms like "concrete repair
Indianapolis" sit in a Map-Pack-dominated SERP the brand cannot yet win. The realistic early
wins are:

1. **City + commercial service long-tails** ("commercial sidewalk repair Westfield IN") — low
   competition, high intent, directly served by the rewritten city/service pages.
2. **Niche commercial service terms** ("dumpster pad replacement", "loading dock pit repair") —
   few local specialists target these explicitly.
3. **Decision-stage informational** ("commercial concrete repair cost", "repair vs replacement")
   — earns links and assists the money pages.

Head-term/Map-Pack visibility is a **P2** that unlocks only after real NAP + GBP + reviews exist
(all flagged REQUIRES HUMAN REVIEW).

## 5. Modifiers library (for natural on-page use, not stuffing)

- **Buyer:** property manager, facility manager, HOA, COA, board, commercial owner, REIT,
  asset manager, general contractor.
- **Asset:** retail center, strip mall, office park, medical/dental, industrial, warehouse,
  distribution center, apartment community, municipal.
- **Problem/spec:** trip hazard, ADA, spalling, settlement, freeze-thaw, expansive clay, joint
  failure, panel replacement, line-item proposal, capital plan, reserve study.
- **Geo:** Hamilton County IN, Indianapolis metro, Carmel, Fishers, Noblesville, Westfield,
  nearby Zionsville, US-31/Meridian, I-69, SR-37, SR-32, Nickel Plate, Grand Park.

## 6. Anti-cannibalization rule

One **primary** keyword per URL, globally unique. Secondaries may overlap loosely but two pages
must never share a primary. The reconciliation against existing inventory is in
`keyword-map.md`; explicit overlap risks are flagged there and governed by
`/seo-audit/url-action-map.md`.
