## Cannibalization flags (must be enforced — see url-action-map.md §guardrails)

1. **`/commercial-concrete-repair` (hub) vs `/concrete-crack-repair` / `/concrete-spalling-repair`
   / `/concrete-joint-repair-sealing`:** the hub must target only the head term and link down.
   Do **not** let the hub rank-target "crack," "spalling," or "joint" primaries. *Highest watch.*
2. **`/parking-lot-concrete-repair` vs `/dumpster-pad-repair`:** the live parking-lot copy
   currently names dumpster pads (`src/data/site.ts:108`). On rewrite, remove dumpster-pad
   targeting from the parking-lot page; it lives on the dumpster-pad page. Cross-link only.
3. **`/sidewalk-repair` vs `/trip-hazard-removal` vs `/ada-compliance-repairs`:** three intents
   (section/panel repair · liability hazard remediation · code compliance). Keep primaries
   distinct; interlink with descriptive anchors, not duplicated bodies/FAQs.
4. **`/warehouse-floor-repair` vs `/concrete-joint-repair-sealing`:** warehouse owns interior
   slab + forklift context; the joint page owns control/expansion-joint repair & sealing
   broadly (incl. exterior pavement joints). Cross-link; don't duplicate joint copy.
5. **`/concrete-ramp-repair` (if built) vs `/ada-compliance-repairs`:** scope ramp page to
   **vehicular/loading** ramps only. ADA pedestrian ramps stay on the ADA page. If clean
   separation can't be written, **don't build** the ramp page.
6. **`/resources/concrete-repair-vs-replacement` (article) vs `/concrete-replacement` &
   `/commercial-concrete-repair` (services):** the article is informational (T3) and must not
   target either service's transactional primary; it links to both.
7. **City pages:** primaries differ only by city — safe — but bodies/FAQs must be genuinely
   local (Phase 4) or they collapse into duplicate-intent doorway pages.
